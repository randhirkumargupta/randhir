Moser Baer Projects

Moser Baer Projects is engaged in the development of power assets using both conventional and non-conventional sources of energy. It is one of the fastest growing integrated power companies in India with an investment plan of over USD 8 billion, across a synergetic span of Thermal, Hydel, Solar and Mining verticals. The company is currently developing 4000 MW of coal based thermal power capacity. It has an advanced pipeline of 500 MW each in the solar and hydro segments.

Its Solar arm - Moser Baer Clean Energy Ltd was incorporated in 2008 with a strategy to undertake development of solar power projects worldwide. It is India's largest project developer, owner and operator of solar power projects with presence in key international markets. The company has 250 MWp under development across multiple states in India and a project portfolio of over 200 MWp in Europe to be developed by 2012. While its first solar project outside India was completed in December, 2009 in Nordendorf, Germany, it commissioned India's first 5 MW solar farm in December 2010 at Sivaganga in Tamil Nadu which is the largest solar farm up and running in India.

Its Hydel arm- Moser Baer Electric Power Ltd was incorporated in 2008 to undertake development of hydro projects sustainably. Currently it is developing hydel power projects with a combined generation capacity of around 410 MW in the state of Himachal Pradesh.

Its Thermal arm - Moser Baer Power & Infrastructures Ltd was incorporated in 2008 with an aim to become a major player in the entire value chain of the thermal power - including generation, transmission and distribution. Currently it is developing thermal power plants in Madhya Pradesh and Chhattisgarh with a combined generation capacity of 4000 MW. Its most advanced thermal power plant based in Madhya Pradesh has already achieved financial closure and acquired fuel linkage.

MBPPL launched its Mining Division in 2008 with the main objective of building a portfolio of technically and economically viable coal assets in India and abroad. It aims to provide fuel security to coal based thermal generation portfolio and explore opportunities besides providing consultancy services to the mineral industry. MBPPL has formed a joint venture with Chhattisgarh Mineral Development Corporation Limited as a partner for development of commercial coal block. The company is currently evaluating several opportunities available in India and abroad.
