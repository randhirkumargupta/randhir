Success Stories

Ankit Sanklecha

A young graduate who was confused like most youngsters his age, makes it big in the online space. Ankit Sanklecha first wanted to take up a banking job, then tried his hand at a supply chain business and when he couldn’t sustain that, his research brought him to the e-commerce world......transforming his life forever. Ankit launched his business of bags & accessories on Snapdeal. In a short span of time, Ankit was at par with the online industry leaders & gained immense recognition for his venture. And guess what- he is only 23 years old. This is Ankit’s story that tells us how e- commerce is nurturing and creating young entrepreneurs across India.

Vikas Nahar

Vikas Nahar from Chennai always knew that joining his father’s toy business was going to be an obvious career decision. Vikas understood the logistics required to run the business & got involved in the traditional style of handling the business. But Vikas soon realised that the business needed a new dimension for expansion & took to e-commerce. This young entrepreneur designed a smart expansion plan for the online space & his business grew like never before. Today, Vikas has made his family proud by gaining recognition in the online toy business & facilitating a massive turnover. Vikas’ story will inspire many youngsters who are looking for a new strategy for business expansion.

Abdul Raheem

Banaras has been the hub for traditional Banarasi saris but consumer preference has changed drastically due to introduction of embroidered saris. As a result, the sari business took a hit & the weavers found it difficult to survive. Amidst the downfall, young Abdul Raheem decided to make a difference & restore the traditional saris. With the support of his wife, Abdul designed a smart online business plan & reached out to customers across the country. In no time, Abdul’s business flourished with the help of e-commerce and amplified the sale of traditional Banarsi saris produced by the traditional weavers. Abdul not only revived a long lost tradition but also helped the weavers who were long forgotten.

Dheeraj Bhatia

Dheeraj Bhatia’s family had owned a toy business in Mumbai for 40 years & it was the obvious choice for Dheeraj to take the legacy forward. Dheeraj was sceptical initially but finally took a decision to partake in the business. However, Dheeraj had a different plan for business expansion & took the e-commerce route to implement his ideas. Dheeraj’s clever thinking has helped him preserve the legacy & gain recognition.

Anshu Sikchi

An HR professional decided to break away from the hectic routine of the corporate world & started her own business after her marriage. Anshu Sikchi from Hyderabad was determined to create her own identity & launch her business of Rajasthani footwear. Anshu had just started her online business when she delivered her baby. Anshu was in a dilemma of managing her baby along with her business & faced immense discouragement. But nothing deterred Anshu’s spirit & she smartly struck a balance between her passion & her responsibilities.

Pravidhi Lakhotia

A mother of two, who belonged to a family where women get bound by marital ties at an early age, aimed to create a niche for herself. Pravidhi Lakhotia had decided at the age of 17 that she wanted to be her own boss. After launching her business at an early age, Pravidhi pursued her dreams even after getting married. She took the e-commerce route & became a successful online entrepreneur in a short span of time.

Darshan Rajpara

A school drop-out, who joined his family business, had bigger plans. Darshan Rajpara of Rajkot yearned to create something innovative & gain immense recognition. He established his business of bathroom & kitchen fittings but urged for further expansion. E-commerce fuelled Darshan’s passion to excel & soon his business soared like never before. Darshan’s story personifies sheer grit & self belief.

Aarti Goyal

A homemaker, who enjoyed designing bed sheets & covers in the comfort of her home decided to make it big in the online industry. As a devoted wife & a mother, Aarti Goyal from Delhi realised that she yearned to do more than just designing for fun. The ecommerce boom gave wings to her yearning & within 5 years her business grew 20 times more. Aarti’s story is an inspiration to many women out there who aim to do well for themselves.

Gaurav Goyal

Gaurav Goyal from Kolkata believes that nothing is impossible. From a single room to an empire, Gaurav strived to achieve his goal of establishing a business of electronic appliances. He did achieve his goal but his hunger to explore new spheres & gain immense recognition was yet to be satisfied. Gaurav took to the ecommerce route & his online sales shot up within 3 months. Gaurav’s drive to do more was the result of his foresight & a never-say-die attitude and the online boom continues to inspire him to excel further.
