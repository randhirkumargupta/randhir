PM NARENDRA MODI'S TRIPS ABROAD

Narendra Modi has visited 18 countries, including two visits to Nepal. He was recently on a trip to China, Mongolia and South Korea. In June 2015, he is slated to visit Dhaka, Bangladesh.

  • Bhutan: Jun 16-17, 2014

  • Brazil: Jul 13-16, 2014

    • Fortaleza for BRICS summit, World Cup

  • Nepal: Aug 3-4, Nov 25-27, 2014

    • First Indian PM to visit Nepal in 17 years.
    • $1 billion credit deal signed between the two countries in the first visit.
    • Delivers a historic speech in Nepal's parliament
    • Visits Kathmandu again in November 2014 for SAARC Summit.

  • Japan: Aug 30 - Sept 3, 2014

    • Japan promised to give $35 billion to India
    • Key areas of investment: Smart cities and Ganges cleanup.
    • Ban lifted on 6 Indian companies, including Hindustan Aeronautics Ltd.

  • USA: Sept 26-30, 2014

    • Visit marks a new direction in strengthening India-US ties.
    • Establishes a personal rapport with President Obama.
    • Modi delivers a momentous speech at Madison Square Garden, New York.

  • Myanmar: Nov 11-13, 2014

    • Attends ASEAN summit
    • Participates in East Asia Summit, in which leaders of 18 countries, including US President Barack Obama and Chinese Premier Li Keqiang take part in Nay Pyi Taw.

  • Australia: Nov 14-18, 2014

    • First Indian PM in Australia after 28 years.
    • Pacts signed on civil nuclear cooperation, social security, transfer of sentenced prisoners, combating narcotics trade, tourism, and arts and culture

  • Fiji: Nov 19, 2014

    • Modi announces a $75 million credit line for Fiji. The two countries agree to expand their defence and security cooperation. First visit by an Indian PM after 33 years.

  • Seychelles: Mar 10-11, 2015

    • Boosts security ties with Seychelles by launching coastal surveillance radar.
    • Agreement signed on hydrographic survey for maritime cooperation.
    • Seychelles leases an island to India for infrastructure development.

  • Mauritius: Mar 11-13, 2015

    • India offered $500 million line of credit for infrastructure projects.
    • Five pacts signed, including on ocean economy.
    • Mauritius offers cooperation on information on taxation.

  • Sri Lanka: Mar 13-14, 2015

    • Four agreements signed on visa, customs, youth development and building Rabindranath Tagore memorial in Sri Lanka
    • RBI and the Central Bank of Sri Lanka enter into a Currency Swap Agreement of $1.5 billion Singapore Mar 29
    • Modi attends state funeral of Singapore's founder and first prime minister Lee Kuan Yew

  • Singapore Mar 29

    • Modi attends state funeral of Singapore's founder and first prime minister Lee Kuan Yew

  • France Apr 9-12

    • Deal for the sale of 36 Rafale fighter aircraft finalised
    • Agreement signed to expedite setting up of Jaitapur Nuclear Power Plant
    • 20 pacts signed covering areas such as civil nuclear energy, urban development, railways and space
    • France pledged 2 billion euros for investment in India
    • Airbus decides to increase outsourcing in India from 400 million euros to 2 billion euros over the next five years.

  • Germany Apr 12-14

    • Inaugurates India Pavillion at the Indo-German Business Summit at the Hannover Fair
    • Pushes Make in India initiative to German CEOs
    • No formal pacts signed, but announcements made to strengthen cooperation in sectors such as energy, skill development, science and technology, Digital India and Clean India.

  • Canada Apr 14-16

    • Modi addresses more than 10,000 people at the Ricoh Coliseum in Toronto
    • Canada's biggest producer of uranium, Cameco, signs a $280 million, five-year deal to supply uranium to fuel Indian nuclear reactors.
    • 900-year-old Khajuraho temple sculpture the Parrot Girl returned by Canada

  • Myanmar: Nov 11-13, 2014

    • Attends ASEAN summit
    • Participates in East Asia Summit, in which leaders of 18 countries, including US President Barack Obama and Chinese Premier Li Keqiang take part in Nay Pyi Taw.

  • China: May 14-16, 2015

    • Visits Xi'an and becomes first world leader to be received outside Beijing
    • Record 24 bilateral agreements signed in Beijing
    • 21 business deals worth $22 billion finalised in Shanghai

  • Mongolia: May 17, 2015

    • Announces $ 1 billion credit line to Mongolia to support its infrastructure

  • South Korea: May 18-19, 2015

    • India, South Korea sign 7 deals, including avoidance of double taxation
    • South Korea to provide $10 billion for India's infrastructure, especially smart cities
