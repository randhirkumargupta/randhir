     HORS D' OEUVRES: DILIP BOBB

Menage a Treo

There is a couple I know who are going through an unusual marital crisis. It's the classic third person in the marriage syndrome except there's a twist to this tale. The third person in the triangle is not another lover but a machine. An Apple MacBook, to be precise. The couple argue constantly over him bringing his laptop and Treo to bed with him. He says it's because his job as the head of an export company means he has to e-mail clients in different time zones. The ménage a trios is a permanent fixture in their marriage and as damaging as an illicit affair.

That's one example of how technology is impacting on personal relationships. With laptops, mobile phones and handheld devices like Blackberry's and Treos getting smaller and more intrusive, they are invading our private spaces like never before. Pychologists attribute this to the changing role of mobile technology. Laptops and hand-held devices are no longer status symbols; they are an unavoidable extension of our lives, and not just official lives. Go to any party and most of the guests will be on their mobiles. We have become hostage to the siren call of the cellphone. People think nothing of cutting short a conversation to take a call. I used to meet interesting people at airports or on flights. No longer: everybody's too busy with their laptops.

Holidays have lost much of their allure as well: you are always on call or busy checking e-mails. A friend from Italy is currently in town. She has a business to run in Rome and two kids to keep track of. The result is that she spends most of her waking hours on her cellphone or her laptop. I am yet to have a decent conversation with her. She, like most parents, says she no longer communicates with her kids, they are constantly engaged with one device or another.

Family, friends, conversation, colleagues, kids, marriage, they are all being sacrificed at the altar of technology. Now that it's entering the bedroom and affecting marital harmony, there's really no personal space left to conquer. The couple I mentioned at the start of this piece are still struggling to come to terms with the third element in their marriage. The wife hasn't given up though: she's determined to find a solution to what she calls the Menage a Treo.
