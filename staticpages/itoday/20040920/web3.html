Adam and Aunt

Incest is most rampant in Chennai. Are we opening up too muchm, asks India Today's Arun Ram.
 

When Prakash broke up with his girl friend in college, his friends either
made fun of him or expressed their sympathies. The 21-year-old liked
neither. Brought up as the only son in a conservative Tamil family, romance
- not to speak of sex - was never a topic to be discussed with the family.
The only exception was his aunt who was "the odd one in." Despite her
prodding him often with enquiries about "girl friends and fun in college,"
Prakash never gave away the secret of his heartthrob and maintained his
"good boy" image.

Now he wanted a shoulder to cry on and the only choice was that of the aunt.
Not only did she not scold her for keeping his affair a secret, but helped
him come out of the distress. "It gets more bearable the next time," she
told him, adding, "don't worry, your next affair will not be too far away."
It wasn't easy for Prakash, but those words helped him believe that moving
from one relationship to another was a normal thing for people his age. True
to his aunt's words, Prakash's next affair wasn't too far away. "It was
sudden," says Prakash "and with my aunt herself."

Prakash knows it is incest. According to most dictionaries, incest is
"sexual intercourse between two people too closely related to marry. "So
what?" says Prakash, "I am not going to marry her." Call it the brashness of
a young male or the courage of the elder female to indulge in `sinful lust,'
sex between relatives are most rampant in the South, especially Chennai.
According to the India Today- AC Nielsen- ORG-MARG sex survey, 23 per cent
of men in Chennai have had sex with a family member, as against the national
average of eight per cent. The corresponding figures for Bangalore and
Hyderabad are 20 and 15 respectively.

"Incest does happen more often than we expect or believe," says Prof. D.
Narayana Reddy, an expert in sexual medicine. "While socio-cultural reasons
are difficult to be attributed to the high incidence of incest in Tamil
Nadu, we can surely say that it is due to the woman opening up, literally
and figuratively. The woman is alone at home as her husband is busy working
and earning. When a younger male relative is readily available incest could
happen. With many families in small town sending their sons to live with a
relative in Chennai, the setting becomes all the more condusive for incest."

Analysts have often attributed incest to the joint family set-up. However,
the new trend seems to be that nuclear families are more likely to be the
stage for incest. The joint family might have offered more opportunities to
get infatuated to a family member, but definitely not the ideal space and
time for the act. Nuclear families, where the woman is alone, provides a
safer option.

Besides incest, are Chennai males the most chauvunistic? Consider these
findings of the survey:
* 54 per cent of men in Chennai find the frequency of sex in their
marriage has decreased after they had had children.


* 35 per cent of men in Chennai feel bored having sex with the same
woman repeatedly.


* 32 per cent (one of the highest in the country) of Chennai males
have had extra-marital sex.
*
* An SMS joke, perhaps, corroborates these findings: Wife, reading an
article, exclaims: "Wow, a bull can have sex 3000 times a year. Can you do
it? Husband replies: "Ask the bull if it is the same cow."
*

* Chennai stands out in many other aspects of the survey, throwing up
a complicated picture. Here are some samples:

* Chennai has the largest percentage (63) of men saying sex is very
important in their life.

* As against the national average of 39 per cent, only 26 per cent in
Chennai would sort out things with their wife if she is found to be
unfaithful.

* More than 35 per cent of males in Chennai have asked for a sexual
favour in return for a job as against the national average of 27 per cent.

* 58 per cent of males in Chennai do not think poorly of women who
agree to have sex easily (National average: 47).

* As against the national average of 71 per cent, only 54 per cent in
Chennai found intelligent women attractive.

* 26 per cent of men in Chennai say they never use condoms, the
highest rate.

* 15 per cent of men in Chennai say they don't bother about their
partner getting an orgasm (National average: 8).

Prof. Reddy is of the opinion that some of the figures show Tamil Nadu in
stark contrast with the North is because we are more outspoken. "Discussions
on sex in the media is the largest in Tamil Nadu. We are more open and
perhaps that explains the high percentages, which could be applicable to
other parts of the country as well. Universally, sexual behaviour and
practice change with attitudes."
That could be ture, but are we heading for an irresponsible, indiscriminate
sexual experimentation? May be the boy in the aunt's bedroom will grow up to
answer in the 2005 survey.
