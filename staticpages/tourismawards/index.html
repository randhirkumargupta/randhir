ADVENTURE
  • Rafting on River Siang, Arunachal Pradesh

    For river sports enthusiasts the thrill of rafting on the Siang River in Arunachal Pradesh is a must have experience.

  • Rann Utsav, The Great Rann of Kutch, Gujarat

    The Rann Utsav held in one of the world’s largest salt deserts gives a glimpse into ethnic arts, crafts, music and dance – a carnival that is more of an adventure experience, to be cherished for a lifetime.

  • Kanchendonzpa National Park, Sikkim

    The Khangchendzonga (High Altitude) National Park, Sikkim is a UNESCO World Heritage site that has spectacular wilderness with Mt. Khangchendzonga towering over it, making it a popular destination for mountaineering, trekking and skiing in the eastern Himalayas.

  • Havelock Beach, Andaman & Nicobar Islands

    Havelock Island is a picturesque natural paradise with white sandy beaches, rich coral reefs and lush green forests makes it a hub for adventure water sports like scuba diving, skiing, sailing, para sailing, wind surfing and snorkelling.

  • River Rafting and Caving, Meghalaya

    Exploring the limestone caves in the Jaintia, Garo Hills and Khasi Hills districts of Meghalaya offers an out-of-the-world experience of stalactites, stalagmites to the adventure lover. Meghalaya is also home to water sports, river rafting on the Dawki and river canyoning at Sohra.

  • White Water Rafting, Rishikesh, Uttarakhand

    Rishikesh, the gateway to the Garhwal Himalayas offers great opportunities for white water rafting on the rapids of the Ganges. Camps.

heritage
  • Tawang Monastery, Tawang, Arunachal Pradesh

    The Gaden Namgyal Lhatse, popularly known as Tawang Monastery is one of the largest Lamaseries of the Mahayana Sect in Asia. It houses the Thankas of Goddess Dri Devi (Palden Lhamo), the principle deity of the monastery.The monastery stands on the spur of a hill 10,000 feet above sea level.

  • Nalanda Vishwavidyalay, Nalanda, Bihar

    Nalanda, founded in the 5th century AD, is the ruins of the world’s most ancient university. Though the Buddha visited Nalanda several times during his lifetime it shot to fame after Hieun Tsang left detailed description of its excellence

  • Takht Shri Harminder Sahib Ji, Patna, Bihar

    Guru Gobind Singh, the tenth Guru of the Sikhs, was born in 1660 in Patna. The Har Mandir Takht, one of the four sacred shrines of the Sikhs, stands at this holy site.

  • Parasnath Temple, Giridih, Jharkhand

    Situated on the highest hill in Jharkhand at an elevation of 4480 feet the Parasnath Temple is considered to be one of the most sanctified holy places of the Jains.

  • Kamakhya Temple, Guwahati, Assam

    The Kamakhya Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to the mother goddess Kamakhya. Situated on the Nilachal Hill near Guwahati city in Assam,it is an important pilgrimage destination for Hindus and especially for Tantric worshipers.

  • Kohima, Nagaland

    Situated in the foothills of the Japfu range Kohima is the land of the Angami Naga tribe. During World War II in the Battle of Kohima the Allies were able to defeat the Japanese and stop their progress. The War Cemetery is maintained by the Commonwealth War Graves Commission .

  • Chardham, Namchi, Sikkim

    Char Dham is a pilgrimage cum cultural complex developed by the Sikkim Government to promote religious, eco and village tourism in the state. It houses an 87-ft statue of Lord Shiva and replicas of the four Dhaams of the country on the Solophok hilltop.

  • Neermahal, Melaghar, Tripura

    Neermahal is a former royal palace built by king Bir Bikram Kishore Debbarman of the erstwhile kingdom of Tripura. The palace is situated in the middle of Rudrasagar lake at Melaghar near Agartala

MOUNTAINS
  • Darjeeling, West Bengal

    Darjeeling in the foothills of the Himalayas has the magnificent Mt. Kanchenjunga as its backdrop and undulating hillsides covered with the emerald green tea bushes as its foreground. It beckons thousands for respite from the bustle of the madding crowds.

  • Aizwal, Mizoram

    Aizwal, the capital of Mizoram, is a bustling city set on the ridges of steep hills. Flanked on the north by craggy peaks of Durtlang it overlooks the valley of river Tlwang.

  • Gulmarg, Jammu & Kashmir

    Gulmarg hill station is a popular skiing destination located in the Pirpanjal range of the Western Himalayas.

  • Wayanad, Kerala

    Wayanad, a little paradise on earth,is amidst lush green vegetation and mist capped hills. It is surrounded by sprawling tea and spice plantations.

  • Shirui Peak, Ukhrul, Manipur

    The main peak of Shirui in Manipur abounds with blooms of Shirui Lily that flowers during monsoon. The exotic Shirui Lily attracts hundreds of scientists and tourists to this veritable paradise.

BEACHES
  • Mandrem, North Goa, Goa

    Mandrem is a white sand beach with clear water in North Goa. Its beauty is during high tide when the water rushes into Mandrem Creek that runs parallel to the coastline.

  • Gopalpur-on-Sea, Odisha

    Gopalpur on sea is the perfect beach destination for a tranquil holiday amidst coconut groves, casuarinas and gentle sand dunes located on the Bay of Bengal coast in southern Odisha. Once a commercial port, it is now a famous tourist destination.

  • Rushikonda Beach, Andhra Pradesh

    Known as the Jewel of the East Coast Rushikonda Beach attracts tourists for its beautiful greenery, golden sands and azure waters. It is also a haven for water sports lovers.

WILDLIFE
  • Betla Forest, Jharkhand

    Betla is a forest of luxuriant sal and bamboo groves, rich in variety of plant and animals. Declared as a national park it boasts of a wide array of wild animals including tiger, leopard, elephant and Indian Gaur amongst others.

  • Lok Tak Lake, Moirang, Manipur

    Loktak lake is the largest fresh water lake in Northeast India famous for it’s phumdis or floating vegetation. It is part of the Keibul Lamjao National Park, the world’s only floating national park that is also home to the endangered Sangai, brown antlered deer.

  • Sunderbans, West Bengal

    The Sunderbans is part of the world’s largest riverine delta formed by the Ganges, Brahmaputra and Meghna. It is the single largest block of tidal halophytic mangrove forest housing India’s largest Tiger Reserve and UNESCO World Heritage site.
