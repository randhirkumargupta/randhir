ODISHA: AN UNSTOPPABLE URGE FOR TRANSFORMATION

Odisha, the most captivating region of India, is often referred as an attractive treasure house of cultures and customs, religion and traditions, languages and literature, art and architecture, scenic beauties and wildlife. State’s varying topography from Eastern Ghats to the fertile river basin of Mahanadi has proven ideal for evolution of compact and unique eco-system. The land, while retaining its pristine glory in all its hues, is marching ahead with inclusive growth in every sector.

Odisha is an agricultural state famous for its traditional art and culture. Since the beginning of the new millennium, the State has been making rapid strides in the field of business, trade and industries. There are significant improvements in different indicators like growth rate, ease-of-doing business and per capita income. We have put in place a progressive industrial policy resolution complemented by various sector specific policies and supported by massive skill development programme, entrepreneurship promotion and infrastructure building.

-Naveen Patnaik CHIEF MINISTER

Odisha is emerging as a manufacturing hub on the east coast of India and among the fastest growing economy in the country. The State has undertaken crucial reforms in various sectors and improved governance, strengthened institutions and streamlined regulatory frameworks. Improving agriculture, irrigation, vital connectivity, electricity, drinking water, health services and quality education for the people of Odisha and empowering them in all their attempts at betterment of their human living conditions have all along been in the priority of the State’s agenda. The State Government has given top priority for development of telecom, electronics, IT and ITES sectors of the state and to work, for Vision 2036, a vision document for holistic development of Odisha in the next 20 years. In the last two decades there has been a remarkable growth of 882% in Odisha’s GSDP. If we compare it with Maharashtra with its GSDP growth rate at 681% and Gujarat at 760% Odisha’s GSDP is far ahead than many other states.

There has been a perceptible and visible change in our State during the last 17 years. Odisha’s agricultural production being doubled in comparison to the year 2000 has been widely acclaimed at national level and the income of farmers has been doubled within the last decade. There is also provision of Crop loans to farmers at 1% interest. Industrial Sector being congenial has attracted many Corporate Houses for investment in Odisha. Odisha is, indeed, transforming, by putting thrust on 3Ts (Team Work, Technology and Transparency) and focusing on 3Is (Irrigation, Inclusion and Infrastructure). Odisha is now more transparent with zero tolerance to corruption. With 80 lakh people moving Above Poverty Line in the last decade, our state is prospering. Within a span of two decades by adopting technology there has been a shift from 54 cr to Rs.3400 cr IT investment and 60% growth in the IT sector. Odisha which was depending on other states for food grain has now become self-sufficient with 3rd largest contributor to India’s Public Distribution System. From birth till death the State Government is providing support to the needy and poorest of the poor. Social security net has been toughened. 24.61 per cent decline has been registered in the poverty level. About 80 lakh people brought out of poverty over the last decade. Steps have been taken for opening of 9 new Government Medical Colleges in the State to create more doctors and meet the growing health care needs of the people. Apart from this, around 1.3 crore people have been provided with free medicines under the ‘Niramaya’ scheme. The State has become a model in the country for ‘Mamata’ Yojana. More than 25 lakh mothers covered with more than 1100 crores of assistance. It is noteworthy that, Infant Mortality Rate in Odisha is lowest in the country even better than the national level. In order to further economic empowerment of WSHG members, the State Government is extending loan @ 1% interest.

Communication infrastructure in rural area has been strengthened through construction of 500 bridges under Biju Setu Yojana. For 5.5 lakh S.T/S.C students more than 6000 Hostels have been made functional. Education loan is being provided at 1% interest to facilitate the students for higher education under Kalinga Siksha Sathi Yojana. Special Development Councils are there for the holistic development of 9 tribal dominated districts of the State. Odisha is a pioneering State in the implementation of Forest Rights Act with 4 lakh Titles to the beneficiaries. Odisha Adarsha Vidyalays are functioning in all 314 Blocks of the State to provide quality CBSE pattern education to the meritorious students in rural areas. Around 19 lakh construction workers got registered and 10 lakh workers distributed with benefits worth Rs.400 crore. “Unnati” a new scheme is launched for holistic development of the urban areas of the State. With the formation of ‘Odisha Skill Development Authority’, ‘Skilled-in-Odisha’ brand introduced to vigorously promote self employment through quality skill development training to the youth of the State. The story of Asian Athletics Championships 2017 is no different. It is the biggest sporting event held in Odisha since 1936. It is a pride for Odisha and Odia people that such a mega event was held in our State. The whole country seemed to shower praise on the Chief Minister of Odisha Shri Naveen Patnaik. This was the first time India surpassed neighbour China in the most successful Athletics campaign. Successful organization of 22nd Asian Athletics Championships-2017 at Kalinga Stadium of Bhubaneswar has put Odisha in the International Sporting Map.

JBVNL- energizing growth and development

Jharkhand Bijli Vitran Nigam Ltd. ,JBVNL , a DISCOM utility of the state of Jharkhand, is directly connected to the consumers of energy availing bodies. Formed on January 6, 2014, after the unbundling of the Jharkhand State Electricity Board, JBVNL took the shape of a Company to maintain power supply across Jharkhand. It maintains power supply in 7 areas and 15 circles to facilitate consumers in their jurisdictions. The areas are Ranchi Electric Supply area, Ranchi; Dhanbad Electric Supply area, Dhanbad; Singhbhum Electric Supply area, Singhbhum; Hazaribagh Electric Supply area, Hazaribagh; Giridih Electric Supply area, Giridih; Dumka Electric Supply area, Dumka and Medininagar Electric Supply area, Medininagar.

Its 15 circles are in Ranchi ,Gumla, Daltonganj, Garhwa, Jamshedpur, Chaibasa, Hazaribagh, Ramgarh, Giridih, Koderma, Dumka, Sahebganj, Deoghar, Dhanbad and Chas. The state of Jharkhand is divided in two parts in terms of the power scenario – DVC Command Area and the non DVC Command Area and JBVNL is mandated to distribute and maintain power supply to the entire area.

Apart from supply and distribution, the following measures are also being augmented for better customer satisfaction and improvement.

  • 1. Ease of doing business
  • 2. Meter to all consumers within 18 months
  • 3. Outsourcing of power stations
  • 4. Strengthening of the Anti Power Theft Cell
  • 5. Induction of Power Portfolio Management.

Energy remains a key requirement in the state of Jharkhand that is drawing up a blue print for rapid industrial growth and economic uplift. JBVNL is right in the forefront providing the spark of development.
