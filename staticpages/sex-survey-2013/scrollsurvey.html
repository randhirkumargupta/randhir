2012: 2012: Sex still a prisoner
The tenth edition of India Today Sex Survey finds that although women are more and more expressive now of their latent desires, yet they still have a long way to go in shedding inhibitions.
2011: Bold & Bored
The ninth edition of the India Today Sex Survey charts the trajectory of India's sex life. The survey, conducted on male and female respondents in the age group 18-60 years across 11 cities, focuses on sex in the family.
2010: Women want more
The India Today survey finds Indian women meditating intently on the prayer beads of fulfilling and wholesome sex. At the root of this pleasure quest could be the post-modern woman's desire to take charge of her own destiny.
2009: The Fantasy Report
The technology of fantasy has expanded dramatically, allowing urban Indians access to a supermarket of choices, but all it has done is expand information, not intimacy. Relationships are less conversation, more convenience; less playgrounds, more prisons.
2008: The naked truth
India Today's survey talks about under-age sex, sex with prostitutes and eunuchs, kinky sex, adultery, incest, homosexuality, fantasies, preferences, favourite positions, attitudes towards role-playing and other sexual activities.
2007: Sex and marriage
The void between the dream of honeymoon and the reality of marriage has to be filled by reclaiming friendship, the birthright of every couple. This India Today survey measures the length and depth of marital issues.
2006: Men in a muddle
Often hypocritical, obsessed with his looks, sexually smug and quietly homoerotic, the single young urban male is surprisingly standard, across big cities and small. An avid consumer of pornography, he has a rich fantasy life, even though his peer group is dominated by males and his dependence on parents substantial.
2005:Sex and the single woman
An exclusive India Today-AC Nielsen-ORG-MARG survey reveals that urban young singleton s are shaking off years of conservatism and asserting their sexuality with growing confidence.
2004: What men want
The nationwide survey reveals some startling findings. As Indian men continue to place purity above passion, a majority of them are sensitive to their partners' fulfilment and do not treat sex as power play.
2003: Sex and the Indian woman
India's first ever, comprehensive, all-female survey that looks at a woman's basic instincts involved interviews with unmarried, married and separated women between 19 and 50 years of age from 10 cities.
