JBVNL- energizing growth and development

Jharkhand Bijli Vitran Nigam Ltd. ,JBVNL , a DISCOM utility of the state of Jharkhand, is directly connected to the consumers of energy availing bodies. Formed on January 6, 2014, after the unbundling of the Jharkhand State Electricity Board, JBVNL took the shape of a Company to maintain power supply across Jharkhand. It maintains power supply in 7 areas and 15 circles to facilitate consumers in their jurisdictions. The areas are Ranchi Electric Supply area, Ranchi; Dhanbad Electric Supply area, Dhanbad; Singhbhum Electric Supply area, Singhbhum; Hazaribagh Electric Supply area, Hazaribagh; Giridih Electric Supply area, Giridih; Dumka Electric Supply area, Dumka and Medininagar Electric Supply area, Medininagar.

Its 15 circles are in Ranchi ,Gumla, Daltonganj, Garhwa, Jamshedpur, Chaibasa, Hazaribagh, Ramgarh, Giridih, Koderma, Dumka, Sahebganj, Deoghar, Dhanbad and Chas. The state of Jharkhand is divided in two parts in terms of the power scenario – DVC Command Area and the non DVC Command Area and JBVNL is mandated to distribute and maintain power supply to the entire area.

Apart from supply and distribution, the following measures are also being augmented for better customer satisfaction and improvement.

  • 1. Ease of doing business
  • 2. Meter to all consumers within 18 months
  • 3. Outsourcing of power stations
  • 4. Strengthening of the Anti Power Theft Cell
  • 5. Induction of Power Portfolio Management.

Energy remains a key requirement in the state of Jharkhand that is drawing up a blue print for rapid industrial growth and economic uplift. JBVNL is right in the forefront providing the spark of development.

Tata Steel

Tata Steel Group is among the top global steel companies with an annual crude steel production capacity of nearly 30 million tonnes per annum (MnTPA) as on March 31,2016. It is now the world's second-most geographically-diversified steel producer, with operations in 26 countries and a commercial presence in over 50 countries. The Tata Steel Group recorded a turnover of US $17.69 billion in FY16. Tata Steel Group has over 76000 employees across five continents and is a Fortune 500 company.

Tata Steel’s global growth began with the objectives of attaining a larger geographic footprint and to service a global customer base, including the mature markets of the UK and Europe and the fast-growing South East Asian and Chinese markets. Apart from its Indian operations, the Tata Steel group today comprises European operations through Tata Steel Europe and South East Asian operations through Tata Steel Thailand and NatSteel Holdings, Singapore.

The Group’s vision is to be the world’s steel industry benchmark in “Value Creation” and “Corporate Citizenship” through the excellence of its people, its innovative approach and overall conduct. Underpinning this vision is a performance culture committed to aspiration targets, safety and social responsibility, continuous improvement, openness and transparency.

After more than a century of growth, the Tata Steel group is well positioned to service a global customer base, to enjoy economies of scale in plant utilisation and research and development and to deliver its ambitious vision with a motivated and highly skilled workforce.

Tata Steel India: Established in 1907, Tata Steel founded and developed India’s first industrial city, now Jamshedpur, where the Company established one of Asia’s first integrated steel plants. The Jamshedpur Works currently comprises a 9.7 MnTPA crude steel production facility and a variety of finishing mills. Tata Steel has a significant presence in allied and downstream areas through its various Strategic Business Units, namely the Tubes Division, Wire Division, Bearings Division, Ferro Alloys and Minerals Division, Industrial By-Products Management Division, Agrico Division and Tata Growth Shop. The Company also possesses and operates captive iron ore, coal and chrome ore mines. The Company dedicated the first phase of its 6 MnTPA greenfield steel project at Kalinganagar to the State of Odisha on November 18,2015. Tata Steel is also examining further capacity enhancement through greenfield projects in Jharkhand, Karnataka, etc.

Tata Steel was founded on the philosophy that the community is not just another stakeholder in business, but is, in fact, the very purpose of its existence. The Corporate Social Responsibility activities of the Company have expanded to over 800 villages in the States of Jharkhand, Odisha and Chhattisgarh.

Jharkhand State Mineral
Development Corporation Ltd
(A Govt. of Jharkhand Undertaking)

The State Government has set up Jharkhand State Mineral Development Corporation Limited on 07th May’ 2002with the following roles:

  • 1. To acquire, take mining lease, prospecting licence, reconnaissance permit, acquire mines, beneficiation plants, concentration and refining plants, acquisition reclamation and improvement of railways, tramways, roads, sewerage, drainage, power supply work, power generation plants, irrigation tubewells, canals, water works, water reservoir, surface water rights.
  • 2. To work on mines and mining rights acquired by it for crushing, quarrying, smelting, refining, dressing, marketing and prepare other value added products from ores, metals, mineral substance, carry out metallurgical operations.
  • 3. To provide technical consultancy services in exploration, mining, process engineering, manufacturing, operating data, plants and other related matters.
  • 4. To encourage joint ventures in the State, Union Territory, other States and abroad with private concerns or corporate bodies in the field of reconnaissance, mapping, prospecting, detailed exploration, mining, beneficiation, metallurgy, mineral processing, manufacture of products, civil construction on mines, power plants and other activities related to its objectives.
  • 5. To enter into long-term agreement for supply of minerals, coal, coal bed methane, processed ore, minerals, raw materials, rocks on long term basis by the said joint ventures or independently.
  • 6. To construct mine entries and carry out all civil work related to mining.
  • 7. Premier producer & supplier of minerals and mineral based products.
  • 8. Core business – Production & Marketing of Coal/ Limestone & Dolomite / Kyanite/ Graphite / Granite Tiles

MISSION

"To Produce & Supply planned quantity of Minerals and Mineral based products with due regards to Safety & Environment, in the interest of People of Jharkhand at large."

Jharcraft

The state of Jharkhand is endowed with rich mineral resources and it has some of the densest forest areas in the sub-continent. This state is also the largest producer of one of the finest quality of silk called tasar. The tasar cocoons are reared and bred in huge quantities by the poor tribal populace in the wild forests of Jharkhand. Our top quality tasar cocoons get optimally utilised by making silk yarn and fabric.

The State Government is involved in the production of high end silk through JHARCRAFT which is a fully owned government corporation. With the design support and assistance of qualified NIFT and NID professionals, JHARCRAFT produces its quality products like silk sarees, running fabrics, stoles, ladies’ apparels, gents’ shirts, neck ties, home & office furnishings and other home decor. Its fabric has Silk Mark, Vanya Silk and Handloom Mark certification. JHARCRAFT also produces cotton handloom fabrics and various handicraft products in Dhokra art, terracotta, cane, bamboo, woodcraft, leather, satin and kantha embroidery, lac, jute, etc. The products manufactured by JHARCRAFT are handcrafted by the poor weavers and other craftspeople of the state.

Jharcraft is providing backward linkage by providing raw materials and other inputs for the above crafts and on the other hand it is providing forward linkage by marketing handloom & handicrafts products at our emporiums situated at different parts of the country. Jharcraft is an implementing agency for various state level and national level government schemes and has also collaborated with many of the renowned organizations for implementation of their CSR activities.

With its ultimate concern for providing employment opportunities to the poor largely tribal population in the rural areas of the state, Jharcraft has expanded its activities through embarked clusters by providing raw materials , design assistance, production support etc. and has brought the local products to the ever expanding market in the country as also in the international market.
