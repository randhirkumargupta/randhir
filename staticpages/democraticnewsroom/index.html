ABOUT DEMOCRATIC NEWSROOM

A #DemocraticNewsroom has many voices but one responsibility - to keep the news honest. We pride ourselves in covering all sides.

In the age where News channels are accused of bias, journalists of running selective agendas, India Today brings you a truly democratic newsroom, one where every shade of opinion is respected and given space. Left, right, centre, in the India Today newsroom, every view is discussed and debated.

We believe in true diversity like the great country in which we live. Which is why no issue is taboo, no opinion ignored, no question unanswered.

Now, in a first of its kind show, we bring the democratic newsroom debates in full public gaze. Join our editors as they discuss, argue, challenge each other on the hot topics of the week.

The Democratic Newsroom: a show like no other, on a channel which prides itself on being the gold standard in journalism.

ABOUT ANCHORS

Politics is his passion and that is evident in his career that has spanned 26 years, RAJDEEP SARDESAI has left no stone unturned and no road unwalked in becoming a voice that is heard when one searches for the unbaised and clear understanding of the political scenario in India. He has come a long way since he graduated in Economics and mastered as a lawyer from Oxford.

His political passion is evident in his best seller 2014: The Election that Changed India. Starting out in TOI, growing into the role of the city editor and steadily making his way to Delhi to become Managing Editor with NDTV before he set up IBN 18 Network with channels like CNN IBN. Today he is the consulting editor with India Today Group and anchors a prime time show on India Today TV.

He has earned many journalism related awards from Best News Presenter to News Anchor of the Year and Padma Shri in 2008 for his unrelenting contribution to journalism.

Rajdeep finds a way to hold onto his original love with print media in ways of the columns he writes for many newspapers including HT.

TVTN Managing Editor Rahul Kanwal has dexterously placed Newsroom on the minds of Indian television viewers.

From news of the day to scoops to debates to interviews to reporting, Rahul Kanwal delivers them all with command, comfort and clarity.

In Newsroom, he gets at the story behind major news events.

Rahul Kanwal is a stand-alone brand in news anchoring for his unscripted and unbiased communication with the guests, live reporters and audiences alike.

A television journalist with an experience of over a decade and half, ANJANA OM KASHYAP is known for her fierce and aggressive stance on issues as she puts the newsmakers and politicians in the dock with the most pointed and pertinent questions. She anchors To The Point on India today along with Halla Bol, Special Report, Rajtilak and Dilli Ke Dil Mein Kya Hai , the most watched and highest TRP shows on Aajtak. She is a bilingual anchor and connects with the viewers in her own unique way, raising their concerns through her shows. She has interviewed the tallest national and international leaders in her grilling way. Recipient of several awards for her journalism, Anjana continues to excel in the television news genre with her in-depth knowledge and research as she has covered various big news events from Ground Zero. On the ground reporting is her forte that gives her deep insight of the real questions to be raised.

GAURAV C SAWANT is Executive Editor India Today. With over 23 years both in print and broadcast journalism Gaurav tracks national security, foreign policy and counter terrorism very closely. His show India First at 8.25 pm focuses on the biggest stories of the day that affect viewers directly. He has won several awards and has authored a book on the Kargil conflict of 1999 – Dateline Kargil: A correspondent’s Nine Week Account from the Battle Front, published by Macmillan. Gaurav has also written the VeerGatha series – on ParamVir Chakra awardees for children.

PADMAJA JOSHI is a News Editor with India Today with 12 years of experience in television. You will find her most comfortable with being where the big story is, and reporting from the middle of the action. Even in the studio, she carries the urge to project the people's voice. Her stories from Dadri, Kathmandu and Kashmir have been widely watched and appreciated. Her body of work spans issues like politics, disasters, agitations; but her heart lies in getting the human stories onto Indian prime time.
