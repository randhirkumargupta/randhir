BLAST FROM THE PAST

  • 2008
    • X
    Vision 20-20:As India's new-age cricketing carnival reached its climax, everyone caught in its undertow could testify that it had really been a heady, hedonistic, all-round good time. No wonder everyone started calling it the cash-rich league. Rajasthan was crowned first IPL champions.
  • 2009
    • X
    League Before Cricket: At three weeks notice, an event involving 59 matches, eight teams, 160-odd cricketers from across the world, 80 support staff and officials, was moved to another country, 7,860 km away. South Africa hosted the show and India got busy with the general elections. Deccan Chargers were crowned the champions in the season.
  • 2010
    • X
    The Controversial One: More than the sport, this edition saw controversies surface. The biggest one was the Modi-Tharoor spat, which ended up with the then Minister of State Shashi Tharoor resigning from his post and the IPL commissioner Lalit Modi being sacked by the BCCI. Chennai tasted their first title win in the season.
  • 2011
    • X
    The League Gets Bigger:Two more teams - Pune and Kochi were added this season making India's much-talked about League all the more exciting. Yuvraj Singh led Pune, while Mahela Jayawardene was the captain of Kochi. Ultimately, MS Dhoni-led Chennai emerged winners.
  • 2012
    • X
    Rise Of New Champions:The season witnessed the termination of Kochi Tuskers Kerala from the tournament. The season was also blemished with a case of spot fixing after 5 players were allegedly caught on a sting operation carried on by a local news channel. Gautam Gambhir-led Kolkata defeated Chennai to become new champions.
  • 2013
    • X
    The Spot-Fixing Saga: The sixth seaon was yet again marred with controversy after the demons of spot-fixing returned to the league. Three high-profile Rajasthan Royals' players were arrested in the infamous scandal raising questions on the credibility of the game. Despite the scandalous uprising, Mumbai Indians clinched their maiden title to give a befitting farewell to the retiring Sachin Tendulkar.
  • 2014
    • X
    Kolkata's Dominance: Following the spot-fixing saga last season, the seventh edition of the IPL finally managed to do away with controversy. The cheerleaders were also removed as part of a plan to focus more on cricket. Yuvraj Singh created a stir after being bought for Rs 14 crore by Bangalore during the auctions. Kolkata defeated Punjab in the final to secure their second title in three years.
