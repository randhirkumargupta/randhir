India at Rio, Highlights: Women's hockey team loss ends woeful Day 3

The Indian contingent had yet another miserable day at the Rio Olympics as its star shooters Abhinav Bindra and Gagan Narang along with both the men's and women's hockey teams suffered heavy defeats on Day 3.

Sattwik Biswal
Mail to author
August 8, 2016
Last Updated: 05:56 IST
New Delhi

Hello and welcome to our LIVE updates from Day 3 of the Rio Olympics 2016. (Rio Olympics live coverage)

Highlights:

3.55 IST: Women's Hockey: Great Britain thrash India 3-0 in their second Group B game. India eves drop down to fourth in the Group B standings, GB top the list.

3.37 IST: Women's Hockey, India vs Great Britain, Pool B: Great Britain lead India 3-0 at the end of the third-quarter. 

3.20 IST: Women's Hockey, India vs Great Britain, Pool B: GOAL! Alexandra Danson adds to India's woes with Great Britain's third goal in the 33rd minute. The match is quickly slipping out of India's grasp.

3.07 IST: Women's Hockey, India vs Great Britain, Pool B: Half-time, Great Britain lead India 2-0 after the end of the first-two quarters. Giselle Ansley and Nicola White scored the goals for GB in the second quarter.

3.03 IST:  Women's Hockey, India vs Great Britain, Pool B: GOAL! Nicola White doubles the lead for GB in the 27th minute of the match. India's defence has been extremely poor so far.

2.59 IST:Women's Hockey, India vs Great Britain, Pool B: GOAL! Great Britain take a 1-0 lead in the second-quarter through a penalty corner. Giselle Ansley scored the goal for GB.

2.48 IST: Women's Hockey, India vs Great Britain, Pool B: First quarter fails to produce a goal for either side with team GB even losing out on a referral for a penalty corner.

2.30 IST: All set for the Women's Hockey encounter between India and Great Britain.

Abhinav Bindra speaks exclusively to India Today:

"All over. I have had a decent career," says Bindra after finishing fourth in 10m Air Rifle final.

"I put my heart, blood and soul into it. I gave my best but it wasn't enough."

Bindra also revealed that the day didn't start off well for him. "The morning didn't start well, the table where my rifle was placed collapsed and the sight broke. I had to go with the alternate one - which is not my best choice," Bindra said.

23:04 IST  India don't have any other events lined up for the day, except for the women's hockey match against Great Britain at 2:30 AM IST.

22:08 IST Sajan Prakash fails to qualify in 200m Butterfly as he finishes outside top 16 in Heats with his timings of 2:09.30.

India on Day 3 so far...

Luck is yet to smile as India are still looking for that elusive first medal. Abhinav Bindra was so close but yet too far as he finished fourth in the 10m Air Rifle  event with Gagan Narang failing to qualify.

In trap qualifications also, Manavjit Sandhu and Kynan Chenai failed to make the cut for the semis. While in men's hockey, India were holding up well against the formidable Germany 1-1 after the Rupinder Pal Singh equaliser in the second quarter.  But with three second remaining to the final whistle, Germany scored the winner to beat India 2-1.

While in individual archery event, Laxmirani Majhi lost to  the Slovakia archer to crash out. In swimming heats also - there was disappointment for India with Shivani Kataria and Sajan Prakash failing.

Next up is Sajan Prakash's Men's 200m Butterfly Heats.

Another big draw would be the men's hockey match between India and Germany in the evening. 

SHOOTING

India have disappointed so far in the shooting events, with the likes of Jitu Rai and Heena Sidhu failing to live up to the expectations. However, India's hopes are now pinned upon shooting stars Abhinav Bindra and Gagan Narang in men's 10m Air Rifle event.

Bindra won an Olympic gold in 2008 Beijing Olympics while Narang will hope to turn his London Games bronze into gold. The qualification starts 17:30 IST, followed by the final at 20:30 IST.

Manavjit Sandhu and Kynan Chenai will also be in action today in the day two of qualifications starting 18:00 IST.

HOCKEY

On a high after an impressive 3-2 win over Ireland in its campaign opener, India's men's hockey team will aim to keep the winning momentum intact against reigning Olympic champions Germany in a keenly anticipated match pool encounter here on Monday.

Against heavyweights Germany, India held their nerve at the recent Champions Trophy where they had an early lead of 3-1 until the third quarter, but defensive errors pegged them back to a draw. It was an inspired performance by the Indian team though Germany were not playing their full squad in that match.

India men's team take on Germany at 19:30 IST, followed by the women's team match against Great Britain at 2:30 (AM) IST.

ARCHERY

Laxmirani Majhi will be in action in the women's 1/32 Eliminations at  19:27 IST.

SWIMMING

Shivani Katari and Sajan Prakash will take part in women's 200m freestyle heats (21:32 IST) and men's 200m butterfly heats (22:04 IST) respectively.
